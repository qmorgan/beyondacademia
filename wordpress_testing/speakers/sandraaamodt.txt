Sandra Aamodt
Science Writer
sandraaamodt.jpg
Sandra Aamodt is the coauthor of two books with Sam Wang, most recently <i>Welcome to Your Child’s Brain: How the Mind Grows from Conception to College.</i> A former editor in chief of <i>Nature Neuroscience</i>, she has read over 5000 neuroscience papers and given talks to scientists, parents, students, and lawyers. She has a bachelor’s degree in biophysics from Johns Hopkins University and a Ph.D. in neuroscience from the University of Rochester and did postdoctoral research at Yale University in the lab of Martha Constantine-Paton. Her science writing has been published in the New York Times, the Washington Post, El Mundo, and the Times of London. Her first book, <i>Welcome to Your Brain: Why You Lose Your Car Keys But Never Forget How to Drive and Other Puzzles of Everyday Life</i>, was named science book of the year in 2009 by the American Association for the Advancement of Science.
